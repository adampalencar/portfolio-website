## One Brand, Asked to Do a Whole Business's Job

Meristem is a self-initiated concept. There was no client; I invented a B2B wholesale plant nursery so I could write a brief worth solving: one brand system, strict enough to spec a one-colour plant tag, carried through to a website a buyer places a trade order on.

Meristem sells to the trade only: landscapers, interior plantscapers, florists, garden centres. They buy by grade and pot size and reorder every week. Every decision in the brand and the site answers to that buyer and the way they order.

## The Category Runs on Spreadsheets

Wholesale nurseries grow beautiful stock and sell it off spreadsheets. Availability, what is ready this week and in what grade, goes out as an emailed PDF that is stale the moment it lands, and there is rarely a way to order online. **The gap is functional.** A buyer cannot see what is actually ready, in what grade, right now.

## One Identity, From the Masthead to the Plant Tag

I built the identity system before the website: a logo, a fixed palette, three type roles, and reproduction rules strict enough to survive a one-colour plant tag. The logo is a seedling, adapted from the Phosphor icon set, because a *meristem* is the growth tip where a plant puts on new tissue. Everything stays flat and geometric so it holds up small and reads as one system.

## A Website the Trade Can Order From

The homepage is the centre of the piece, because a supplier's site is where the money moves. The hero states the offer, and this week's stock sits one tap away in a preview card. The real work is the ledger below it.

<figure class="cs-figure cs-figure--full">
  <Image src={homepageImg} alt="Meristem homepage: text navigation with a trade-account call to action, the Plants properly supplied hero, and a floating This Week preview card listing three lots with status chips and a link to the full list" />
  <figcaption class="cs-figure__caption">Homepage &middot; the offer up top, this week's stock one tap from the full list</figcaption>
</figure>

<figure class="cs-figure cs-figure--full">
  <Image src={availabilityLedgerImg} alt="Meristem availability ledger: a filterable trade list with category chips, and rows carrying botanical and common names, pot size, height, grade, quantity price breaks, and colour-coded status chips for available, limited, and booking" />
  <figcaption class="cs-figure__caption">Availability ledger &middot; filter, check status, add to the order</figcaption>
</figure>

Each row shows what a buyer decides on: botanical and common name, pot size, height, grade, a quantity price break, and a status. Available, Limited, and Booking each get their own colour and their own action, so a sold-out lot offers to book the next run instead of pretending it can ship.

<figure class="cs-figure cs-figure--full">
  <Image src={interactionImg} alt="Availability interaction states: a row on hover surfaces an Add button, an added row shows a terracotta rail with a quantity stepper set to 24, and the module foot reconciles a running one-lot, twenty-four-unit order total" />
  <figcaption class="cs-figure__caption">Interaction &middot; hover to add, commit with a stepper, watch the order total tally</figcaption>
</figure>

Hover a row and the add control appears. Add a lot and it commits with a terracotta rail and a quantity stepper, while the foot of the module keeps a running total. **I built ordering to fill in like a delivery sheet**, one line at a time, closer to trade paperwork than a consumer checkout.

<figure class="cs-figure cs-figure--full">
  <Image src={categoriesImg} alt="Categories band: six browse cards using the identity glyphs for Foliage, Perennials, Trees and Shrubs, Ornamental Grasses, Succulents and Cacti, and Groundcover, each with a short description and a view availability link" />
  <figcaption class="cs-figure__caption">Category cards &middot; the identity glyphs reused as the site's filters</figcaption>
</figure>

The site runs on real text navigation, category cards built from the identity glyphs, and a footer carrying the EN and FR toggle a Canadian trade supplier needs. The layout reflows to a phone, where the ledger stacks instead of breaking. Same palette, same type, same glyphs as the identity sheet, now running a live interface a buyer can order from.

## What I Set Out to Prove

Meristem starts as an identity and ends as a working order tool. **The hard part was the website**: making a week's availability something a trade buyer can read, trust, and order from in one visit, in the brand's own voice. Print collateral and running content channels are work I already do for real clients, so I kept the concept to what it proves best, the identity and the site. The business is invented, Phosphor is credited wherever the logo appears, and I have tried to claim only what the piece actually shows.

If I ran it for real, the first thing I would watch is whether that availability ledger turns site visits into opened trade accounts, and I would tune the system from there.
